Informatics Society of Central Europe

2025 Annual Symposium

“Bridging Theory and Practice in the Digital Age”
October 15–17, 2025  •  Corvinus University of Budapest, Hungary
Schedule Keynotes Tracks Chairs Venue Committee

Welcome to the 2025 Annual Symposium of the Informatics Society of Central Europe. This three-day gathering brings together researchers, practitioners, and policymakers from across the region to share cutting-edge work in artificial intelligence, information systems, cybersecurity, and data science. We are honoured to host this year’s edition in Budapest, a city at the crossroads of European innovation and tradition.

Keynote Speakers

Prof. Dr. Mária Kovács

Eötvös Loránd University (ELTE), Budapest, Hungary
Keynote: “Explainable AI in High-Stakes Decision Making: Lessons from European Healthcare”

Mária Kovács is Professor of Computer Science at ELTE and director of the Budapest AI Research Lab. She holds a PhD from the Technical University of Vienna and has published over 150 papers on interpretable machine learning. She chaired the European Commission’s High-Level Expert Group on AI Transparency (2022–2024) and is a recipient of the Humboldt Research Award. Her current work focuses on post-hoc explanation methods for deep neural networks deployed in clinical settings.

Prof. Dr. Piotr Zieliński

Warsaw University of Technology, Poland
Keynote: “Zero-Trust Architectures for Critical Infrastructure in Central Europe”

Piotr Zieliński is Head of the Cybersecurity Research Group at the Warsaw University of Technology and a senior advisor to the Polish National Cyber Security Centre. He earned his doctorate at the University of Cambridge and has led multiple EU Horizon projects on resilient network design. His research spans intrusion detection, software-defined perimeter security, and cyber threat intelligence sharing frameworks across the Visegrád Group countries.

Dr. Elena Stoyanova

Sofia University “St. Kliment Ohridski”, Bulgaria
Keynote: “Federated Data Science: Privacy-Preserving Analytics at Scale”

Elena Stoyanova is Associate Professor of Statistics and Data Science at Sofia University. Previously a research scientist at the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems, her work on federated learning and differential privacy has been adopted by Eurostat for cross-border statistical disclosure control. She serves on the editorial board of the Journal of Privacy and Confidentiality and co-leads the Balkan Data Science Network.

Venue & Practical Information

Conference Venue

Corvinus University of Budapest
Fővám tér 8, 1093 Budapest, Hungary

The main campus is situated along the Danube river in central Budapest, directly opposite the Great Market Hall (Nagy Vásárcsarnok) and within walking distance of the Liberty Bridge (Szabadság híd).

Getting There

By Metro: M4 line to Fővám tér station (exit directly at the campus).
By Tram: Lines 2, 47, 48, 49 to Fővám tér.
From Airport (BUD): Bus 100E to Deák Ferenc tér, then M3/M2/M4 metro (~45 minutes).
Taxi: Főtaxi official airport partner; ~9,500 HUF (~25 EUR).
