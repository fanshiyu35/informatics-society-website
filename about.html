About the Society

The Informatics Society was founded in 2014 by a group of researchers and practitioners committed to advancing the interdisciplinary field of information science. Headquartered in Budapest, Hungary, the Society operates as an independent, non-profit learned society with members across Europe, Asia, and North America.

Mission

Our mission is to foster excellence in information science research, promote the exchange of knowledge across disciplinary boundaries, and support the professional development of our members through publications, conferences, and collaborative initiatives.

Governance

The Society is governed by an Executive Board elected by the membership. Day-to-day operations are managed by the Secretariat. The Society's activities are organized through specialized Communities, each with its own steering committee and program of activities.

History

  • 2014 — Society founded in Budapest
  • 2016 — First Annual Symposium held (Budapest)
  • 2018 — Launch of the Society's peer-reviewed proceedings series
  • 2020 — Expansion to online seminars and virtual conferences
  • 2022 — Membership exceeds 200 researchers
  • 2024 — AI Community established; Information Systems Research Forum launched
  • 2026 — Preparation for 12th Annual Symposium

Membership

Membership is open to researchers, educators, and practitioners with an active interest in information science and its applications. Members receive access to Society publications, discounted registration for events, and opportunities to serve on review committees and working groups.
