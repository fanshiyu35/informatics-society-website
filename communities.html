Specialized Communities

The Society's activities are organized through specialized communities, each focused on a specific area of information science research and practice. Communities organize seminars, workshops, peer review activities, and collaborative research projects.

Artificial Intelligence Community

Established: 2024

The AI Community brings together researchers working on artificial intelligence, machine learning, knowledge representation, natural language processing, and the societal implications of AI technologies. The Community places particular emphasis on the intersection of AI with information systems, accounting informatics, and business intelligence.

The Community organizes an annual workshop, monthly research seminars, and a peer review program for the Society's publications and symposium submissions.

Current Focus Areas:

  • Machine Learning for Business Analytics
  • Knowledge Representation and Reasoning
  • Natural Language Processing
  • AI Ethics and Governance
  • Accounting Information Systems and AI

Information Systems Research Community

Established: 2024

This Community focuses on the design, implementation, and evaluation of information systems in organizational contexts. Research areas include enterprise systems, business process management, data analytics, and the socio-technical aspects of technology adoption.

Cybersecurity Community

Established: 2016

The Cybersecurity Community addresses the technical, organizational, and policy dimensions of information security. Members contribute to research on threat intelligence, secure system design, and privacy-preserving technologies.

Data Science Community

Established: 2018

The Data Science Community explores methodologies for extracting knowledge from structured and unstructured data. Research spans statistical learning, data mining, visualization, and big data architectures.
